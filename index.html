Science Structures Test - April 16

Study Notes

  • Structure: Something that has a specific shape and size and serves a purpose or function.
  • Types of Structures:
    • Frame: Made from a skeleton (e.g., bridge, jungle gym)
    • Shell: Hollow with a strong outside (e.g., helmet, egg)
    • Solid: Made from solid materials (e.g., brick, rock)
    • Complex: Combination of the above (e.g., airplane)
  • External Forces: Forces acting from outside the structure
    • Compression: Squishing
    • Tension: Stretching
    • Torsion: Twisting
    • Shear: Sliding layers
  • Force Descriptions:
    • Direction: Where the force goes
    • Magnitude: How strong the force is
    • Plane of Application: The surface the force acts on
    • Point of Application: The exact location the force hits
  • Internal Forces: Forces acting within the structure
  • Centre of Gravity: The point where the weight is balanced
  • Structural Symmetry: Balanced shape that helps with stability
  • Reasons Structures Fail:
    • Overloaded
    • Bad design
    • Faulty construction
    • Extreme conditions
    • Poor foundation
  • Material Choice Factors: Cost, strength, weight, flexibility
  • Preventing Structural Failure: Good design, testing, proper materials
  • Ergonomics: Designing for human use and comfort
  • Ergonomics helps: Everyone, especially workers, students, and users
